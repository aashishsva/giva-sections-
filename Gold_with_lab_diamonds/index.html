More about the flowers ▼
  • Mountain Ebony - Native to India, Kempu Mandara (Kannada) or Kachnar (Hindi) is a feast for the senses and is found in vibrant shades of pink. Its delicate petals have been celebrated for their skin-soothing properties for centuries.
  • Eyebright - Found in Europe and North America, the delicate white petals of Eyebright are marked with purple veins and a splash of yellow. This flower has been used for centuries as a herbal medicine to improve eye health.
  • Bell Mallow - Native to Maharashtra, the critically endangered Son Ghanta (Bell Mallow) flower is a rare and precious sight. Its golden yellow petals with purple veins are a delight to behold.
  • Trillium - Found in North America and Asia, Trillium, the wildflower with three iconic petals, is a symbol of recovery, beauty, and purity. This rare and endangered flower is a reminder of the power of nature to renew itself.
  • Safed Musli - Found in Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh, a revered flower in Ayurvedic traditions, the herb is often called a divya aushadh (divine medicine). It is celebrated for its potential revitalising properties.
  • Brahma Kamal - Found all over the Western Ghats, the Brahma Kamal is a sacred flower that blooms only once a year, for a single night. Its intoxicating scent is said to transport the mind to a higher plane.
